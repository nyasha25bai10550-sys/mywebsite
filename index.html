poem 8

When I say I don’t care, I lie,
When I say I didn’t wait, I try.

You walked away, I wished you well.
So why should I still care at all,
When you were the one who chose to fall?
I hate you no, I hate me too,
You who left, me waiting for you.
You made a fool of what we were,
And you loved me? Yeah sure.
I laughed out loud, then stood so still,
A broken heart, a frozen will.
“Never leaving,” you once swore,
Funny how you closed the door.
Did I move on? I wish I’d know—
Maybe yes
Maybe no.
